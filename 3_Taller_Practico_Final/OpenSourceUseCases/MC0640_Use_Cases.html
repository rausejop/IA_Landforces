§ 1 · Executive Summary

What this report contains and how to read it

This Technical Report presents 38 Use Cases for the Land Tactical CIS profile PFL-00403, drawn from the Information Exchange Requirement tables of Annex C to Draft MC 0640. UC-001..UC-032 cover the steady-state Brigade-and-below catalogue; UC-033..UC-038 are tactical Land IERs anchored in the new doctrine of the Allied Reaction Force (ARF) operating under MC 0593 across the three Regional Plans (Norte · Centro · Sur) and through Major Joint Operations Adjacent. Every UC is generated under the NAF v4 + ADatP-47 methodology, grounded in AJP-5 (Operational Planning), AJP-6 (CIS) and AJP-01 (Allied Joint Doctrine), and overlaid with the FMN Spiral 6 technical realities (Data-Centric Security · Attribute-Based Encryption · STANAG 4606 Ed. 4 protected tactical communications · accredited unmanned sensor federations).

Every UC card carries two parts: a NAF v4 · ADatP-47 Specification Header (formal IER ID, primary actor, goal, trigger, pre/postconditions, NAF view, FMN service, releasability, doctrinal basis) and a 14-row Operational Body (the six IER dimensions plus equipment, time window, environment, zones, availability, classification, risk meter, and attack surface). The "Copy" button on each card emits a Word-ready HTML table — paste directly into a Word document.

Scope

Brigade · Battle Group · Company. MC 0640 covers LANDCOM downward; this report focuses on Bde-and-below. Upper echelons (Division, Corps, LANDCOM) appear only as upstream reach-back context.

Method

NAF v4 NOV/NSOV views + ADatP-47 SysML-style UC packages. AJP-5 supplies commander's intent; AJP-6 mandates the service-oriented stance. STEADFAST COBALT supplies the technical constraints.

Realisation

FMN Spiral 5 / NISP Baseline 16 services on a typical NATO stack: Systematic SitaWare on Klas Voyager, routed by Cisco ESR6300, into Thales Synaps / L3Harris AN/PRC-163 SDRs, with Advenica / Fox-IT CDS at every NNI.

2.3 — Why "deterministic"

Determinism here means three things:

  • Schema-fixed. Every UC has the exact same 16-field NAF header + 14-row body (see §8 Use Case Framework). No structural variation between UCs.
  • Rule-derived. Field values are produced by explicit derivation rules from the canonical inputs. The IER ID, FMN service, NAF view, releasability, and C3 Taxonomy L1/L2 are computed; only operational specifics (goal, trigger, pre/postconditions, attack-surface narrative) are hand-curated and per-UC traceable.
  • Reference-traceable. Every technical assertion either cites a Reference (A–R in §11) or names a STANAG / AJP / RFC inline. The Annex B formal-definitions table is the dictionary against which every term in this report can be checked.

§ 4 · Introduction & Doctrinal Basis

What an IER is, and why every Use Case picks one

IER — Information Exchange Requirement

Per AJP-6, an IER is a category of information that must be exchanged between operational facilities so commanders can decide. MC 0640 makes each IER answer six dimensions: Who, What, When, Protection, Precedence, Volume. In this project we treat each row of Annex C as one Serial (S-NNN) paired 1:1 with one Use Case (UC-NNN).

14-row Use Case card

Each UC card renders 14 fields in fixed order — six IER dimensions, then equipment, time window, environment, zones/CoI, availability, classification, an LPI/LPD risk meter with a one-sentence narrative, and an attack-surface note. Mission classification overrides the IER's notional default.

Use Case generation standard — NAF v4 + ADatP-47

In NATO, Use Cases are not authored ad-hoc. They are generated under the NATO Architecture Framework (NAF) v4 and detailed procedurally by ADatP-47 (Allied Data Publication 47). AJPs supply the doctrinal context (AJP-5 for operational planning, AJP-6 for CIS), not the UC template. Every card in §05 carries a NAF v4 · ADatP-47 spec header above the 14-row operational body.

LayerStandardRole for UC generation
Mother frameworkNAF v4Defines NOV (Operational View) + NSOV (Service-Oriented View). UCs are the bridge from operational need to technical solution.
Procedural data std.ADatP-47Working-group rules for interoperability design. Operational UCs drafted in SysML / UML-adapted military methodology.
Op. planning doctrineAJP-5Commander's intent + lines of operation. Engineers convert these into technical UCs.

Anti-pattern: citing an AJP as the UC origin. The UC origin is the NAF view + ADatP-47 model; AJPs justify it.

NATO MC framework — joint umbrella and four tactical components

In NATO doctrine, CIS and C2 capability requirements are architected through a structured multi-domain framework. MC 0593 is the strategic + operational capstone — the joint umbrella that establishes the baseline interoperability standards, data-sharing protocols, and transversal cybersecurity requirements for Federated Mission Networking (FMN). Beneath it, three domain-specific tactical Military Committee directives codify CIS at the field level: MC 0640 for Land, MC 0195 for Maritime, and MC 0655 for Air. This deliverable visualises only MC 0640 (Brigade and below); LANDCOM, Corps and Division appear as upstream reach-back nodes whose primary UC content belongs to MC 0593.

Cyberspace is governed transversally through AJP-3.20 (Allied Joint Doctrine for Cyberspace Operations), relying on the core security architecture mandated by MC 0593 and managed operationally by the NATO Computer Incident Response Capability (NCIRC) to guarantee mission assurance across the combined joint enterprise.

§ 5 · Technical Constraints — STEADFAST COBALT lessons learned

Four constraint blocks that shape every Use Case

1. Day-Zero Protected Core Networking

National units down to Brigade level must not route raw IP across boundaries. All cross-NNI data rides STANAG 4711 IPsec PCN tunnels over an agnostic black transport backbone. Every Brigade spins up a PCR mapped into the FMN Mission Network Element; NNI dynamic routing uses BGP-4.

STANAG 4711PCRBGP-4NNIBlack transport

2. Echelon-tiered bandwidth & protocol

Corps/Div HQ: SATCOM + fibre, IPv4/IPv6 dual-stack, MIP4 baseline, full HD video. Bde/Bn: MarLOS STANAG 4691 + SATURN Ed.4 PRF-151 FFH for air-ground. Coy/Plt: XMPP for chat, AComP-5634 MELPe 2.4 kbps VARC half-duplex PTT. Standard VoIP is blocked at the edge.

STANAG 4691 MarLOSSATURN Ed.4STANAG 5651 ESSORSTANAG 5634 NBWFAComP-5634 MELPeXMPP RFC 6120

3. NNI data integrity — STANAG 4774 labels + CDS

"Weakest link" doctrine: a single tactical-CP compromise at a small nation can traverse the federated core. Inter-nation boundaries enforce Cross-Domain Solutions with STANAG 4774 confidentiality metadata labels. NFFI (STANAG 5527) BFT updates from lower-classification networks must pass label validation before ingress to NATO Secret COPs.

STANAG 4774STANAG 5527 NFFICDSXML metadata label

4. SWaP-C — NFV / DCIS at the edge

Migration off heavy single-purpose racks to Network Functions Virtualization on Deployable CIS nodes. Coy-to-Bn Command Posts run on a single COTS ruggedized SFF blade hosting routing engine, XMPP server, local C2 registry, and security gateway. Stand-up target: under 30 minutes.

§ 11 · Conclusions & Findings

What this Technical Report demonstrates, and what remains open

This Technical Report has applied the NAF v4 + ADatP-47 framework deterministically to the Information Exchange Requirement tables of Draft MC 0640 Annex C, producing 32 representative Use Cases across the three echelons in scope (Company · Battle Group · Brigade). Each UC carries the full formal specification header (16 fields per §8.1) and the 14-row operational body (§8.2), and is realisable on the typical NATO ground stack documented in §6 and Annex A.

11.1 — Coverage assessment

Echelon coverage

All three MC 0640 tactical bands at 38 UCs total: Company (11 UCs — voice + chat + BFT + fires + CASEVAC + recce + ARF-context coastal CT), Battle Group (11 UCs — CP stand-up + BFT + formal messaging + VTC + multinational coordination + cross-national CFF + RSOMI under GPS denial), Brigade (16 UCs — fires + intel + airspace + EW + sustainment + UAS ISR + coastal observation push to ARF Maritime MNE + GBAD RAP segment + Bde-to-Bde Strategic Swing hand-off). UC-033..UC-038 are tactical Land IERs anchored in ARF / Regional Plan / MJO-Adjacent scenarios (ARCTIC SENTINEL · BALTIC SHIELD · MEDITERRANEAN GUARDIAN · BLACK SEA HORIZON · CYBER REACH-BACK · STRATEGIC SWING) with the FMN Spiral 6 overlay (DCS · ABE · STANAG 4606 Ed. 4).

Service coverage

The FMN Spiral 5 / PFL-00403 service catalog is exercised across Voice (Secure), Tactical Messaging (XMPP), Military Messaging (APP-11 MTF), Situational Awareness (NFFI / BFT), Targeting (AEP-76 / JDSSDM Overlay+Contact), Environmental (METEOC / JDSSDM GenInfo), VTC + Office Collab, Logistics, EW Services, Information Management / Document Collab, Core Networking (PCN), and Airspace Management.

Threat coverage

Risk meter spans 1/5 (garrison RF) to 5/5 (LPI+LPD mission-critical under EW). The four STEADFAST COBALT constraint blocks (PCN · echelon-tiered bandwidth · STANAG 4774 NNI labels · NFV/DCIS) are mapped onto every UC's row 7 (Equipment) and row 14 (Attack Surface).

11.2 — Key findings

  • Bearer tier maps cleanly to echelon. Company-level UCs land predominantly on NBWF (VHF 30–88 MHz / UHF 225–400 MHz, 25 kHz channels) with MELPe-2400. BG/Bde UCs land on ESSOR HDR (UHF/L-band 225 MHz – 2.5 GHz) with SATCOM uplink for higher reach-back. No UC required a bearer outside the NBWF/ESSOR/SATURN/SATCOM matrix.
  • PCN is the single point of trust at BDE-and-up. STANAG 4711 IPsec PCN + BGP-4 federation appears as a precondition in 18 of 32 UCs. PCR compromise would cascade through every dependent UC — addressed via TCP-AO authentication and RPKI-style origin validation.
  • STANAG 4774 label enforcement is the federation-critical control. Cross-domain releasability decisions (NS → NR/NU degradation) recur in 7 UCs spanning intel ingress, multinational BFT, and air-ground coordination. Advenica / Fox-IT CDS appliances are the named realisation in Annex A.
  • NFV stand-up time is the operational tempo gate. The STEADFAST COBALT <30-minute target appears explicitly in UC-002 (BG CP stand-up, <20 min target) and UC-032 (Coy NFV blade restore, <30 min). The Klas Voyager + Cisco ESR6300 + Systematic SitaWare Edge stack is the de-facto realisation.
  • Vendor consolidation around four blocks. The 32 NATO nations procure across the four blocks listed in §6 (SDR · NFV · C2 software · CDS). Annex A confirms a typical UC requires at least one vendor from each of Block 1, 2 and 3; Block 4 (CDS) appears only in cross-domain UCs.

11.3 — Open work & future iterations

  • Validation status: all UCs are currently DRAFT v0.1. Ratification by LANDCOM CCIS Branch and the FMN community of interest is required before any UC moves to APPROVED.
  • Annex C coverage: the 32 UCs sample the inventory of 1,339 persisted Annex C Serials (extraction exhausts the source PDF; Air Support / Medical / Non-NATO sections are absent from this copy of Draft MC 0640). The UC ↔ Serial mapping is persisted as serials/uc_serial_map.json: 24 of the 32 UCs anchor to a canonical Annex C row, 4 are unmapped (Medical / restore / UAS-as-source / BG-VTC gaps), 7 anchor labels were corrected against the canonical JSON, and the §10 Serial sample was regenerated row-by-row from the JSON. A full reverse index (every Annex C Serial → UC) is the next iteration.
  • PFL-00403 enrichment: every one of the 1,339 Serials now carries an enrich:{…} field — typical bearer, frequency band, STANAG anchors, LPI/LPD base, default availability, and attack-surface theme — derived from a 4 tier × 12 category matrix in serials/enrichment_dictionary.json and persisted as serials/annexC_enriched.json. Coverage is 100% (every entity in the extraction and every IE Product is mapped). STANAG anchors are derived from STEADFAST COBALT + the canonical UCs and still need cross-validation against the formal NISP Baseline 16 PFL-00403 entry.
  • NISP B16 alignment audit (2026-05-22): verified against the canonical Service Area CI-1062 inventory. PFL-00403 (the deliverable's anchor) is confirmed as Land Tactical C2 IE Profile (FMN Spiral 5), mandating AEP-76 Edition A Version 3 (2023) Volumes I–V under STANAG 4677 Ed.1, with the JDSSDM 1.2 message set on the JDSS Gateway in exclusive mode (BR BACK010). PFL-00446 "IP Access to Tactical Radio (FMN Spiral 5)" is confirmed as the canonical edge-IP profile cited in Coy-band UCs (mandates AEP-76 Volume V). A separate finding: many UC equipment lines reference "MIP4" generically — strictly, PFL-00403 uses MIP 3.1 XML extended with JDSSDM 1.2 (not MIP4). MIP4 = PFL-00402 is a peer profile in the same Spiral 5; PFL-00405 mediates between them. Future iteration: tighten Coy / BG card text to read "AEP-76 / JDSSDM (STANAG 4677)" where the canonical anchor is JDSS rather than MIP4.
  • UAS data-link characterisation (UC-028): the platform-specific L/C-band data link is intentionally generic. Per-platform refinement (e.g. specific NATO RPAS Initial Capability waveforms) belongs to a follow-on iteration.
  • Bilateral / national-specific bearers (e.g. national C2 stacks of Nations C/D in UC-016) are abstracted in this report. A nation-specific edition can substitute Systematic SitaWare with national C2 stacks while keeping the FMN-side profile identical.

FFHFast Frequency HoppingSTANAG 4372 (SATURN) · AJP-3.6Electronic-protection technique in which the transmitter and receiver pseudo-randomly change carrier frequency many times per second following a shared hop pattern. Baseline for SATURN and integral to NBWF / ESSOR HDR EPM modes.

LPD: low probability that the receiver detects an emission is present at all. Typically achieved through spread-spectrum, frequency hopping, and low-duty-cycle techniques.
MarLOSMulti-channel Ad-hoc Relay · Line-Of-SightSTANAG 4691NATO standard for a multi-channel tactical line-of-sight relay capable of operating in an ad-hoc mesh topology, for medium-bandwidth packetised IP at brigade and battalion level.
MELPeMixed Excitation Linear Prediction enhancedSTANAG 4591 (codec) · STANAG 5634 / AComP-5634 (NBWF profile)A low-rate (typically 600, 1200, or 2400 bit/s) speech codec adopted by NATO for narrowband secure-voice channels. PTTPush-To-TalkSTANAG 5634 / AComP-5634A half-duplex voice control mode in which the operator must press and hold a button to transmit; the universal control paradigm of tactical radio voice. STANAG 5634 / AComP-5634 defines the VARC half-duplex signalling for MELPe over NBWF.
